Laith's Portfolio

Photography

My photography can be found at Cheddareness.com. I used to ask my siblings and parents for their old film cameras, since it would give me an excuse to go out and explore the world. While I do not do too much film photography now (because the film and processing of the film has gotten very expensive), I spent a considerable amount of time exploring the world through my photography. I eventually displayed my photos in a few exhibitions across Los Angeles.





Visuals

As a producer and DJ of Techno music, I create visuals to support the music. The “face” video uses the AI site “thispersondoesnotexist” to evoke being among strangers at a rave. I also built a TouchDesigner system reactive to the kick: when the kick hits, distortion/glitch floods the screen to mirror the intensity of the track. Lastly, I built a visual around a 3D scan of my face, driven live by my drum machine and acid bass via Ableton → TouchDesigner.





Videos

I’ve shot music videos and VHS-style experiments using thrifted cameras. During my first Deltopia at UCSB, I documented the day with a rapid edit to match the pace of the crowd. For my music, I recorded a Trance DJ set and edited a playful visual to make the viewing/listening experience more fun. The full set is on YouTube; the clip here is a teaser.
